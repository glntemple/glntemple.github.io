Temple History

A Sacred Journey Through Time

Origins and Establishment

The Shri Gopalakrishna Lakshminarasimha Temple in Pallipady has a profound history that spans several generations. Originally under Jain rule, the temple later came under the administration of Brahmin families, who performed important rituals like Navaratri and Ananta Chaturdashi. The sacred temple has long been a cornerstone of the local community's spiritual life.

Dedicated to both Lord Krishna and Lord Narasimha, the temple holds great religious importance in the region. Its architecture blends traditional South Indian temple styles, drawing influences from both Karnataka and Kerala, reflecting its deep-rooted heritage and cultural significance.

Key Milestones

MidAge

Jain rule, later Brahmin settlement.

1934

Flood destroyed temple; rebuilt by Subbarao & family.

1980s

Flood damaged temple again; rebuilt by Sri Shankaranarayana Rao and locals; idols found under banyan tree.

2020

Temple reconstruction started.

2022

Temple reconstruction completed.
